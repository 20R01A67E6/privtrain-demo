The Problem

AI is powerful.
But your data is sensitive.

When you use ChatGPT or Claude with real business data — patient records, legal documents, financial reports — you're sending that data to someone else's servers. For many industries, that's not just risky. It's illegal.

🏥

A hospital can't use cloud AI

HIPAA regulations mean patient records cannot be sent to external servers. One breach = millions in fines and loss of patient trust.

⚖️

A law firm can't share case files

Attorney-client privilege means confidential case documents cannot leave the firm's control — ever. Bar association rules make this non-negotiable.

🏦

A bank can't expose financial data

Internal financial models, client portfolios, and trading strategies are market-sensitive. Sending them to a third-party AI is a compliance nightmare.

Simple analogy

Think of it like hiring a specialist vs. asking a stranger

Using ChatGPT for sensitive data is like explaining your confidential business problem loudly in a public café. PrivaTrain is like hiring a dedicated expert who works exclusively for you, inside your office, who gets better at your specific problems every day — and never tells anyone else what they know.

❌ Cloud AI Problem

Your sensitive data leaves your building, goes through the internet, and is processed on someone else's computer. You have no idea what happens to it.

✅ PrivaTrain Solution

The AI model comes to your data. Training happens in an encrypted container. Only the trained model (not your data) is returned to you.

Every example gets a priority score

Before training begins, a scoring system analyses each piece of data and decides how valuable it is. This prevents wasting compute on data the model already knows, and flags contradictions before they cause problems.

Why this matters: Without scoring, you might retrain on 1,000 examples the model already handles perfectly. That wastes GPU time and money. Only high-priority examples trigger training.

Old and new data get blended intelligently

AI has a problem called "catastrophic forgetting" — if you train it only on new data, it forgets everything it learned before. PrivaTrain mixes old and new data in the right proportions automatically.

Simple analogy: Imagine learning French but forgetting all your English. That's catastrophic forgetting. The data mixer makes sure the AI keeps what it knew while learning what's new.

Training runs on rented GPUs — privately

Your encrypted training job is sent to a GPU cloud provider. Your raw data never leaves your machine — only the encrypted job payload does. The trained model weights are returned to you.

Why GPU rental? Training AI requires powerful graphics cards that most businesses don't own. Renting GPU time costs $2–15 per training job — far cheaper than buying hardware.

New model must prove it's better

Before your current model is replaced, the new one is put through a set of benchmark tests. If it scores better, it gets deployed. If not, it's blocked and the diagnostician explains what went wrong.

Why this matters: Without evaluation, a bad training run could silently make your model worse. No other fine-tuning tool has this automatic safety gate built in.

Every use makes the model smarter

When users mark an answer as wrong, or correct the model, that feedback is logged. Over time the platform identifies patterns in failures, collects enough new signal, and automatically triggers the pipeline again — making the model better without anyone pressing a button.

This is the agentic loop: The model monitors its own performance, decides when retraining is worthwhile, and improves itself. No competitor offers this for private local AI.
