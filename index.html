The Problem

AI is powerful.
But your data is sensitive.

When you use ChatGPT or Claude with real business data — patient records, legal documents, financial reports — you're sending that data to someone else's servers. For many industries, that's not just risky. It's illegal.

🏥

A hospital can't use cloud AI

HIPAA regulations mean patient records cannot be sent to external servers. One breach = millions in fines and loss of patient trust.

⚖️

A law firm can't share case files

Attorney-client privilege means confidential case documents cannot leave the firm's control — ever. Bar association rules make this non-negotiable.

🏦

A bank can't expose financial data

Internal financial models, client portfolios, and trading strategies are market-sensitive. Sending them to a third-party AI is a compliance nightmare.

Simple analogy

Think of it like hiring a specialist vs. asking a stranger

Using ChatGPT for sensitive data is like explaining your confidential business problem loudly in a public café. PrivaTrain is like hiring a dedicated expert who works exclusively for you, inside your office, who gets better at your specific problems every day — and never tells anyone else what they know.

❌ Cloud AI Problem

Your sensitive data leaves your building, goes through the internet, and is processed on someone else's computer. You have no idea what happens to it.

✅ PrivaTrain Solution

The AI model comes to your data. Training happens in an encrypted container. Only the trained model (not your data) is returned to you.

Every example gets a priority score

Before training begins, a scoring system analyses each piece of data and decides how valuable it is. Contradictions are flagged before they cause problems.

Why this matters: Without scoring, you might retrain on 1,000 examples the model already handles perfectly — wasting GPU time and money.

Old and new data get blended intelligently

AI has a problem called "catastrophic forgetting" — training only on new data makes it forget everything it learned before. PrivaTrain mixes data in the right proportions automatically.

Simple analogy: Imagine learning French but forgetting all your English. The data mixer prevents that.

Training runs on rented GPUs — privately

Your encrypted training job is sent to a GPU cloud provider. Raw data never leaves your machine. The trained model weights are returned to you.

Why GPU rental? Training AI requires powerful graphics cards most businesses don't own. Renting costs $2–15 per job — far cheaper than buying hardware.
Training job — live

New model must prove it's better

Before your current model is replaced, the new one runs benchmark tests. If it scores better, it gets deployed. If not, it's blocked and the diagnostician explains what went wrong.

Why this matters: Without evaluation, a bad training run could silently make your model worse. No other fine-tuning tool has this gate built in.

Every use makes the model smarter

When users mark an answer as wrong, that feedback is logged. The platform identifies failure patterns, collects enough signal, and automatically triggers the pipeline again — no button needed.

This is the agentic loop: The model monitors its own performance and improves itself. No competitor offers this for private local AI.
